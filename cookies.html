1. What are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, as well as to provide information to the owners of the site. Think of them as tiny memory aids for your browser, helping the website remember you and your preferences for a smoother, more personalized experience. They play a crucial role in modern web browsing, enabling many of the features we've come to expect from online services.

Cookies perform various functions, such as remembering your login details, storing items in your shopping cart, or helping us understand how you interact with our content. They can be "session cookies" which are temporary and deleted when you close your browser, or "persistent cookies" which remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them. This distinction is important because persistent cookies allow websites to remember your preferences or actions across multiple visits, enhancing continuity.

4. Your Choices Regarding Cookies – Your Kitchen, Your Rules

You have the power to decide whether to accept or decline cookies. Most web browsers automatically accept cookies, but you can usually modify your browser setting to decline cookies if you prefer. This might prevent you from taking full advantage of the website. Think of it as adjusting the settings on your oven – you have control over how things are cooked.

4.1. Managing Cookies Through Your Browser

You can manage cookies directly through your web browser settings. Each browser offers different methods for controlling cookies. Here's a general guide:

  • Google Chrome: Go to Settings > Privacy and security > Site Settings > Cookies and site data.
  • Mozilla Firefox: Go to Options > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data.
  • Microsoft Edge: Go to Settings > Privacy, search, and services > Clear browsing data or Cookies and site permissions.
  • Safari: Go to Preferences > Privacy.

You can typically:

  • Delete all cookies.
  • Block all cookies.
  • Allow all cookies.
  • Block third-party cookies.
  • Clear specific cookies.
  • Receive a notification before a cookie is set.

Please note that if you choose to disable cookies, some features of our Site may not function as intended. For example, you might not be able to log in, save preferences, or use interactive tools effectively. This is because many functionalities rely on cookies to remember your actions and settings.

4.2. Opt-Out of Specific Services

Some third-party services that use cookies offer their own opt-out mechanisms. For example:

  • Google Analytics: You can install the Google Analytics opt-out browser add-on to prevent your data from being used by Google Analytics.
  • Ad Networks: Many advertising networks are part of initiatives like the Network Advertising Initiative (NAI) or the Digital Advertising Alliance (DAA), which offer centralized opt-out tools for participating companies.

We encourage you to explore these options if you wish to have more granular control over specific types of cookies.
