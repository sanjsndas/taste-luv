1. Information We Collect

We may collect various types of information from Users in a variety of ways, including, but not limited to, when Users visit our site, register on the site, place an order, subscribe to the newsletter, respond to a survey, fill out a form, and in connection with other activities, services, features, or resources we make available on our Site. 1.1. Personal Identification Information

This refers to information that can be used to identify you directly or indirectly. We may collect personal identification information from Users only if they voluntarily submit such information to us. This may include:

  • Name: When you create an account, comment on a blog post, or submit a recipe, we may ask for your first and last name. This helps us personalize your experience and attribute your contributions.
  • Email Address: This is primarily collected for account registration, newsletter subscriptions, contact form submissions, and for sending you updates, promotional materials (if you opt-in), or important service-related communications. Your email is a key identifier for your account.

  • Phone Number: In rare cases, such as for order fulfillment or customer support, we might request a phone number, but this is always optional unless explicitly required for a specific service.
  • Profile Information: If you choose to create a user profile, you might provide additional details such as a profile picture, a short bio, or preferences related to recipes and content. This information is typically optional and designed to enhance your community experience.
  • Demographic Information: While not typically collected directly, if you participate in surveys, we might ask for non-identifiable demographic data (e.g., age range, cooking experience level) to help us understand our audience better and tailor content. This is always optional and aggregated.

Users can always refuse to supply personal identification information, except that it may prevent them from engaging in certain Site-related activities. For example, you cannot subscribe to a newsletter without providing an email address, or create a personalized journal without some form of identification.

1.2. Non-Personal Identification Information

We may collect non-personal identification information about Users whenever they interact with our Site. Non-personal identification information may include the browser name, the type of computer, and technical information about Users' means of connection to our Site, such as the operating system and the Internet service providers utilized and other similar information.   • Operating System: To optimize our site for various devices and software.
  • IP Address: Used for general location data (e.g., country) and to help with security and fraud prevention. IP addresses are typically dynamic and do not directly identify an individual.
  • Referral Source: The website or link that brought you to Tasteluv.com, helping us understand our traffic sources.
  • Pages Visited: Which pages you navigate to on our site, and the order in which you view them.
  • Time Spent on Pages: How long you spend on each page, indicating content engagement.
  • Clickstream Data: The path you take through our website, clicks, and interactions.
  • Device Information: Such as device type (mobile, tablet, desktop) and screen resolution, to optimize for different viewing experiences.

This non-personal data is crucial for analytical purposes, allowing us to enhance site performance, user experience, and content relevance without compromising individual privacy. We analyze this data in aggregate to identify trends and patterns, ensuring our site remains a delightful and efficient resource for all users.

Our Site may use "cookies" to enhance User experience. User's web browser places cookies on their hard drive for record-keeping purposes and sometimes to track information about them. You can find a more detailed explanation in our dedicated Cookie Policy. In brief, cookies are small text files stored on your device that help us:

  • Remember Your Preferences: Such as language settings or login status, so you don't have to re-enter them.
  • Analyze Site Usage: To understand which parts of our site are most popular and how users interact with them, helping us improve content and navigation.
  • Personalize Content: To show you recipes or blog posts that might be more relevant to your interests based on past interactions.
  • Enable Functionality: For features like "Tasteluv Journal Generator" or "Cook Together" to function correctly.
  • Advertising: To deliver more relevant advertisements (if applicable) based on your browsing habits, though we prioritize non-intrusive advertising that aligns with our brand values.

Users may choose to set their web browser to refuse cookies, or to alert you when cookies are being sent. If they do so, note that some parts of the Site may not function properly. For detailed instructions on managing cookies, please refer to your browser's help documentation or our Cookie Policy. We respect your choice to manage your cookie preferences and aim to provide a functional experience regardless of your settings, though certain personalized features may be limited.

3. How We Protect Your Information

We adopt appropriate data collection, storage, and processing practices and security measures to protect against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction of your personal information, username, password, transaction information, and data stored on our Site. The security of your data is paramount to us, reflecting our commitment to maintaining a trustworthy and safe online environment for your culinary journey.

  • Encryption: We use Secure Sockets Layer (SSL) technology to encrypt data transmitted between your browser and our servers.   • Access Control: Access to your personal data is restricted to employees, contractors, and agents who need to know that information in order to operate, develop, or improve our services. These individuals are bound by strict confidentiality obligations and may be subject to discipline, including termination and criminal prosecution, if they fail to meet these obligations.
  • Regular Security Audits: We conduct regular security audits and vulnerability assessments of our systems and infrastructure to identify and address potential weaknesses proactively. This continuous monitoring helps us stay ahead of emerging threats and maintain a robust security posture.
  • Data Minimization: We adhere to the principle of data minimization, collecting only the information that is necessary for the purposes outlined in this Privacy Policy. We do not collect excessive or irrelevant data, reducing the risk associated with data storage.
  • Data Anonymization and Aggregation: Whenever possible, and especially for analytical purposes, we anonymize or aggregate data so that it cannot be linked back to individual users. This protects your privacy while still allowing us to derive valuable insights.
  • Physical Security: Our servers and data centers are housed in secure facilities with restricted access, surveillance, and other physical security measures to prevent unauthorized entry and protect hardware.
  • Backup and Recovery: We implement robust data backup and recovery procedures to prevent data loss in the event of system failures or disasters. This ensures that your information remains available and intact.
  • Employee Training: All Tasteluv employees who handle personal data undergo regular training on data privacy and security best practices. This ensures that our team is aware of their responsibilities and equipped to handle your information with the utmost care.
  • Third-Party Vendor Security: When we engage third-party service providers who may handle your data (e.g., payment processors, hosting providers), we conduct due diligence to ensure they adhere to high security standards and comply with relevant data protection regulations. We enter into data processing agreements where necessary to safeguard your information.
  • Incident Response Plan: In the unlikely event of a data breach or security incident, we have a comprehensive incident response plan in place. This plan outlines procedures for detection, containment, investigation, notification (where legally required), and remediation, ensuring a swift and effective response to minimize any potential impact.

While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your Personal Information, no method of transmission over the Internet or method of electronic storage is 100% secure. Therefore, we cannot guarantee its absolute security. We continuously review and update our security practices to adapt to new technologies and threats, ensuring the highest possible level of protection for your data.

7. Your Data Protection Rights (GDPR & CCPA Considerations)

Depending on your location, you may have certain rights regarding your personal data. Tasteluv is committed to facilitating the exercise of these rights. While this is a general overview, specific rights may vary based on applicable laws (e.g., General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) for EU residents, California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) for California residents).

7.1. Right to Access

You have the right to request copies of your personal data that we hold. This allows you to verify the accuracy of the information and ensure it is being processed lawfully. We will provide this information in a clear, concise, and easily understandable format. You can request details about the categories of personal data collected, the purposes of processing, the categories of recipients, and the retention periods.

7.2. Right to Rectification

You have the right to request that Tasteluv correct any information you believe is inaccurate or incomplete. If you find any discrepancies in your profile or other data, please contact us, and we will promptly update it. 7.3. Right to Erasure (Right to Be Forgotten)

You have the right to request that Tasteluv erase your personal data under certain conditions. These conditions include: when the data is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, when you withdraw consent, when you object to processing and there are no overriding legitimate grounds, or when the data has been unlawfully processed. Please note that certain data may need to be retained for legal or legitimate business purposes, such as transaction records or compliance with regulatory obligations.

7.4. Right to Restrict Processing

You have the right to request that Tasteluv restrict the processing of your personal data under certain conditions. This means that we would store your data but not process it further. This right applies when: you contest the accuracy of the data (for a period allowing us to verify accuracy), the processing is unlawful and you oppose erasure, we no longer need the data for processing but you require it for legal claims, or you have objected to processing pending verification of legitimate grounds.

7.5. Right to Object to Processing

You have the right to object to Tasteluv's processing of your personal data, particularly when the processing is based on our legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes. If you object, we will cease processing your data unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds for the processing that override your interests, rights, and freedoms, or for the establishment, exercise, or defense of legal claims. For direct marketing, your objection will always be honored.

7.6. Right to Data Portability

You have the right to request that Tasteluv transfer the data that we have collected to another organization, or directly to you, under certain conditions. This applies to data processed by automated means where you provided consent or where processing is necessary for a contract. We will provide your data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format.

7.7. Right to Withdraw Consent

Where our processing of your personal data is based on your consent, you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time. Withdrawal of consent will not affect the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal. This means that if you previously gave us permission to use your data for a specific purpose, you can revoke that permission at any time, and we will cease that specific processing activity.

7.8. Right to Lodge a Complaint

You have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority if you believe that our processing of your personal data infringes applicable data protection laws. We encourage you to contact us first so we can attempt to resolve your concerns directly, but you always have the right to approach the relevant authority.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the contact information provided at the end of this Privacy Policy.
